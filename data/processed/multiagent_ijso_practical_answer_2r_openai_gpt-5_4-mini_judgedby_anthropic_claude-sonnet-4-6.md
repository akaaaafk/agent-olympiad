## **III. Determination of vitamin C concentration in cabbage juice**

**III–1. Half-equations in acidic medium**

\[
\mathrm{IO_3^- + 6H^+ + 6e^- \rightarrow I^- + 3H_2O}

\[
\mathrm{2I^- \rightarrow I_2 + 2e^-}
\]

**III–2. Overall equation between iodate and iodide ions**
\[
\mathrm{IO_3^- + 5I^- + 6H^+ \rightarrow 3I_2 + 3H_2O}
\]

**III–3. Titration table**  
Use the **experimental burette readings** and calculate the titre:
\[
V_{\text{KIO}_3} = V_{\text{final}} - V_{\text{initial}}
\]

**III–4. Moles of iodate that reacted**
\[
n(\mathrm{IO_3^-}) = C \times V
\]
where
\[
C = 0.002\ \mathrm{mol\,L^{-1}}
\]
and \(V\) is the titre volume in L.

**III–5. Moles of iodine formed**
From the equation:
\[
1\ \mathrm{mol\ IO_3^-} \rightarrow 3\ \mathrm{mol\ I_2}

so
\[
n(\mathrm{I_2}) = 3n(\mathrm{IO_3^-})

**III–6. Moles of ascorbic acid in the cabbage juice sample**

**V–2. Geometry**

**V–4. Effective resistance**
